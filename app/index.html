About Me

Raised by pig farmers in new guiena, my childhood was a troubled one. I often found myself with our fat potbelly pigs as my only friends, I affectionely reffered to them as Chocolate and Chip. I didn't really attend school, my father had spent a few years working at the butcher shop so he knew his numbers, and my momma was a rebel book smuggler (see, women weren't really supposed to read back then), so she taught me my letters by candlelight each night. I loved the farm, it was a beautiful place in the country, and my worries were few. One day in my 18th summer, papa told me it was time to forge a new life for myself. Sadly, doing so required the slaughter of Chocolate and Chip to fund my ticket out.
